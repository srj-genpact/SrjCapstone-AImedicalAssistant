# Clinical Practice Guidelines: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM)

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is diagnosed based on any of the following parameters:
- **Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)**: >= 6.5% (must be confirmed with repeat test unless symptoms are present).
- **Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)**: >= 126 mg/dL (after no caloric intake for at least 8 hours).
- **2-Hour Postprandial Glucose**: >= 200 mg/dL during an Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) using 75g glucose.
- **Random Plasma Glucose**: >= 200 mg/dL in a patient with classic symptoms of hyperglycemia (polyuria, polydipsia, unexplained weight loss).

## 2. Glycemic Goals
Target levels should be individualized, but general recommendations are:
- **HbA1c**: < 7.0% for most non-pregnant adults.
- **Preprandial Capillary Glucose**: 80–130 mg/dL.
- **Peak Postprandial Capillary Glucose**: < 180 mg/dL (1–2 hours after a meal).

## 3. Pharmacological Management
- **First-Line Therapy**: Metformin is the preferred initial pharmacological agent in the absence of contraindications (e.g., eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²).
- **Combination Therapy**: If the baseline HbA1c is >= 1.5% above target, start with dual therapy.
  - **Cardiovascular/Renal Protection**: In patients with established ASCVD, Heart Failure (HF), or CKD, add an SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Empagliflozin) or GLP-1 receptor agonist (e.g., Semaglutide) with proven benefit.
  - **Weight Loss Focus**: GLP-1 receptor agonists or dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., Tirzepatide) are preferred for high weight-loss efficacy.
  - **Hypoglycemia Avoidance**: DP4 inhibitors, SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 receptor agonists, or Thiazolidinediones (TZDs) minimize hypoglycemia risk.
- **Insulin Therapy**: Consider early insulin initiation if there is evidence of ongoing catabolism (weight loss), symptoms of hyperglycemia are present, or HbA1c is > 10% or blood glucose is >= 300 mg/dL.

## 4. Monitoring and Screening
- Check HbA1c twice yearly in patients meeting treatment goals. Check quarterly in patients whose therapy has changed or who are not meeting glycemic goals.
- Annual screening for diabetic complications: microalbuminuria (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio), nephropathy (eGFR), retinopathy (dilated eye exam), and neuropathy (comprehensive foot exam).
