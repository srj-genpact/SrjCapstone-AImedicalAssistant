# Clinical Practice Guidelines: Asthma Management (GINA-Aligned)

## 1. Diagnosis and Assessment
Asthma is characterized by chronic airway inflammation. Key diagnostic features:
- History of variable respiratory symptoms (wheeze, shortness of breath, chest tightness, cough) that vary in intensity over time.
- Documented expiratory airflow limitation (e.g., FEV1/FVC ratio is reduced to < 0.75-0.80 in adults, and bronchodilator reversibility shows an increase in FEV1 by > 12% and > 200 mL).

## 2. Asthma Severity Staging
Severity is assessed retrospectively based on the level of treatment required to control symptoms:
- **Mild Asthma**: Well-controlled with Step 1 or Step 2 treatment.
- **Moderate Asthma**: Well-controlled with Step 3 or Step 4 treatment.
- **Severe Asthma**: Requires Step 5 treatment (high-dose ICS-LABA + biologic/systemic steroid) or remains uncontrolled despite it.

## 3. Stepwise Pharmacological Treatment
Pharmacological treatment should be adjusted dynamically based on control status:
- **Preferred Rescue/Controller (GINA Track 1)**: Formoterol (a rapid-onset long-acting beta-agonist) combined with an Inhaled Corticosteroid (ICS), such as Budesonide-Formoterol, used as-needed for symptom relief in Steps 1 and 2, and as Maintenance and Reliever Therapy (MART) in Steps 3–5.
- **Alternative Approach (Track 2)**:
  - **Step 1**: SABA (e.g., Albuterol) as-needed rescue inhaler, taken along with a low-dose ICS whenever SABA is taken.
  - **Step 2**: Low-dose daily ICS (e.g., Fluticasone propionate) + as-needed SABA.
  - **Step 3**: Low-dose daily ICS-LABA (e.g., Fluticasone-Salmeterol) + as-needed SABA.
  - **Step 4**: Medium-dose daily ICS-LABA + as-needed SABA.
  - **Step 5**: High-dose daily ICS-LABA + add-on treatments (e.g., LAMA like Tiotropium, anti-IgE biologic, or low-dose oral corticosteroids).

## 4. Trigger Management and Action Plan
- Identify and avoid environmental triggers (pollen, dust mites, mold, animal dander, tobacco smoke, cold air).
- Provide every patient with a written **Asthma Action Plan** indicating:
  - **Green Zone (Controlled)**: Daily controller medications.
  - **Yellow Zone (Exacerbation Warning)**: Increase rescue inhaler use, consider temporary oral corticosteroid course.
  - **Red Zone (Medical Emergency)**: Frequent rescue inhaler use, call emergency services immediately if no improvement.
