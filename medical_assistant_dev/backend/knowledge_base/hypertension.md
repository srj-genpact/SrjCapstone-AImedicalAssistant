# Clinical Practice Guidelines: Hypertension Management

## 1. Classification of Blood Pressure (BP)
Blood pressure is categorized into four stages for adults (aged 18 and older):
- **Normal**: Systolic BP < 120 mmHg and Diastolic BP < 80 mmHg.
- **Elevated**: Systolic BP 120–129 mmHg and Diastolic BP < 80 mmHg.
- **Stage 1 Hypertension**: Systolic BP 130–139 mmHg or Diastolic BP 80–89 mmHg.
- **Stage 2 Hypertension**: Systolic BP >= 140 mmHg or Diastolic BP >= 90 mmHg.

## 2. Lifestyle Modifications
All patients with elevated BP or hypertension should be advised on non-pharmacological interventions:
- **Sodium Restriction**: Reduce dietary sodium intake to < 2,300 mg/day (ideally < 1,500 mg/day).
- **DASH Diet**: Adopt a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy.
- **Physical Activity**: Engage in 90–150 minutes of aerobic exercise per week.
- **Weight Loss**: Aim for a healthy body weight (BMI 18.5–24.9). Expect a 1 mmHg BP reduction per 1 kg of weight loss.
- **Alcohol Limitation**: Limit alcohol to <= 2 drinks/day for men and <= 1 drink/day for women.

## 3. Pharmacological Management
For Stage 1 Hypertension with a 10-year ASCVD risk >= 10%, or Stage 2 Hypertension, initiate BP-lowering medication:
- **First-Line Agents**: Thiazide diuretics (e.g., Chlorthalidone, Hydrochlorothiazide), ACE inhibitors (e.g., Lisinopril), ARBs (e.g., Losartan), or Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs, e.g., Amlodipine).
- **Special Conditions**:
  - **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)**: Use an ACE inhibitor or ARB as first-line therapy to preserve renal function.
  - **Diabetes Mellitus**: ACE inhibitors or ARBs are strongly recommended due to renal protection.
  - **Black Patients**: Thiazide diuretics or CCBs are generally more effective as initial monotherapy in the absence of CKD or heart failure.
- **Combination Therapy**: Start with two first-line agents from different classes for Stage 2 Hypertension if BP is > 20/10 mmHg above target.

## 4. Monitoring and Follow-Up
- Re-evaluate blood pressure 1 month after initiating or adjusting therapy.
- Once BP goals (< 130/80 mmHg) are met, follow up every 3 to 6 months.
