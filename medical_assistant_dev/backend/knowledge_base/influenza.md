# Clinical Practice Guidelines: Influenza Management

## 1. Clinical Presentation and Symptoms
Influenza (flu) is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses (Type A and B). Common clinical features:
- Sudden onset of fever or chills.
- Cough, sore throat, nasal congestion, or runny nose.
- Muscle pain, body aches, headache, and severe fatigue.
- Gastrointestinal symptoms (vomiting, diarrhea) can occur, more commonly in children.

## 2. Diagnostic Testing
- **Clinical Diagnosis**: During peak influenza season, a clinical diagnosis can be made based on fever and cough.
- **Rapid Influenza Diagnostic Tests (RIDTs)**: Antigen detection assays that provide results in < 15 minutes, but have moderate sensitivity (50–70%). A negative RIDT does not rule out influenza.
- **Molecular Assays (RT-PCR)**: High-sensitivity and high-specificity tests. Recommended for hospitalized or high-risk patients.

## 3. Pharmacological Treatment
- **Antiviral Therapy**: Neuraminidase inhibitors (e.g., Oseltamivir/Tamiflu) should be initiated as soon as possible.
  - **Timing**: Most effective when started within **48 hours** of symptom onset.
  - **Indications**:
    - **Hospitalized Patients**: Treat immediately.
    - **High-Risk Individuals**: Initiate treatment early for children < 2 years, adults >= 65 years, pregnant individuals, and patients with chronic medical conditions (asthma, COPD, diabetes, heart disease, immunosuppression).
    - **Healthy Patients**: Antiviral therapy can be considered for previously healthy outpatients presenting within 48 hours to shorten symptom duration by approximately 1 day.
  - **Standard Dosage (Oseltamivir)**: 75 mg orally twice daily for 5 days in adults.

## 4. Supportive Care and Prevention
- **Symptom Relief**: Rest, hydration, and antipyretics (e.g., Acetaminophen, Ibuprofen) to control fever and pain. Avoid Aspirin in children/teens due to risk of Reye's Syndrome.
- **Prevention**: Annual vaccination for everyone aged 6 months and older is the primary method of prevention.
